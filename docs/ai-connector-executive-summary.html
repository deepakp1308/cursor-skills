Strategic Implication

The AI connectors deliver strong engagement and Automations adoption among existing paid users — a genuine competitive advantage over QBO. But they are failing as acquisition channels: zero meaningful paid conversion from new signups, which even the weakest traditional channel achieves at 7–10%.

The Analytics Agent is the single highest-leverage fix. Connector users are already Mailchimp's most engaged cohort — outperforming in-app analytics users on WAU, Automations, SMS, and churn at every plan tier. But without the agent, they underperform on the exact actions it drives: email sends (−12 pts) and segment creation (−2 pts). The paid conversion gap is the most revealing evidence: channels with the agent convert at 99.9%; without it, 0.5%.

Embedding the Analytics Agent in the connector would: (1) close the email and segment gap for 5,500 already-engaged users, (2) create an upgrade trigger for 3,028 free users who currently have zero reason to convert, (3) push Standard users toward Premium-level behavior, and (4) compound the retention advantage into the lowest-churn cohort on the platform.

Claude is the stronger channel on every forward-looking metric. Prioritize embedding the Analytics Agent in Claude first — it has the fastest-growing, most-engaged user base and the highest ceiling for incremental payoff.
