User Composition: Existing Accounts, Not New Customers

Over 90% of both ChatGPT and Claude connections on Mailchimp are existing accounts. Of the connected user base, Claude skews more toward paid plans (63.4% paid) than ChatGPT (52.6% paid) — both far above any other Mailchimp integration channel (Shopify: 10%, Canva: 18%). These are power users adding AI tools to active accounts, not new customers discovering Mailchimp.

Strategic Implication

The AI connectors deliver strong engagement and Automations adoption among existing paid users — a genuine competitive advantage over QBO. But they are failing completely as acquisition channels: zero meaningful paid conversion from new signups, which even the weakest traditional channel achieves at 7–10%.

Claude is the stronger channel on every forward-looking metric (velocity, engagement, Automations attachment), while ChatGPT is declining and should be monitored for intervention. The key decision: invest in fixing the free-to-paid funnel for AI-sourced signups, or formally reposition these channels as retention/engagement tools and measure them accordingly.
